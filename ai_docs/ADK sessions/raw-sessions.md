Executive Summary
This investigation provides a comprehensive overview of Anthropic AI, an American artificial intelligence startup established in 2021. The company was founded by former OpenAI employees, including siblings Dario and Daniela Amodei, who departed due to differing views on AI safety and development direction [1, 2, 3]. Anthropic operates as a public-benefit corporation, with a stated mission to develop AI systems that are safe, reliable, interpretable, and beneficial to humanity. This commitment to safety is a core aspect of its corporate identity and research focus [1, 3, 4, 5, 6, 11].

Anthropic has secured its position as a major player in the AI landscape through significant financial backing from major tech companies, including investments of up to $4 billion from Amazon and $2 billion from Google [1, 2, 3]. The company's primary products are the Claude chatbot and its family of large language models (LLMs), which include tiers such as Opus, Sonnet, and Haiku, each designed for varying levels of complexity and computational efficiency [1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 11]. To further its mission, Anthropic actively engages in AI safety research and has launched initiatives like "Labs" for incubating experimental products and the "Economic Index" to measure the real-world impact of its technology [5, 9, 10, 11].
